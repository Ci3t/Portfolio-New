## Main Content Opportunity

The CV is slightly outdated and reads like a standard resume. The portfolio should convert it into a sharper narrative:

> Full-stack developer with a UI-first eye, AI SaaS experience, and instructor-level communication.

That combination is stronger than "full stack developer" alone. It gives HR simple keywords and gives engineers proof that Rani can explain systems, work across frontend/backend, and ship product surfaces.

## Reference Design Strengths

- Strong first impression with huge RANI ALI type.
- Distinctive dark/noir system identity.
- Good font pairing: Syne for display, Hanken Grotesk for body, JetBrains Mono for technical labels.
- Sharp grids and terminal labels create a recognizable portfolio voice.
- The portrait zone creates a premium editorial feel.
- Existing nav terms like Deployments, Chronicles, Uplink are memorable.

## Reference Design Risks

- The copy is currently too generic: "elite collaborators demanding precision and scale" sounds dramatic but not specific.
- The "LAT 40.7128 / LON -74.0060" placeholder points to New York and should be replaced or removed.
- Too much terminal language could reduce clarity for HR if every label becomes cryptic.
- Scroll snapping can feel slick on desktop but annoying on mobile or when content height varies.
- The design is currently only a hero. The rest needs equally strong concepts so the site does not peak on load.

## Recommended Positioning

Use a dual-language mental model:

- Public role: Frontend / Full Stack Developer with UI focus
- Secondary angle: AI product builder and full-stack instructor
- Internal theme: premium dark interface with deployment/system details

Recruiter-readable copy should remain clear. The noir/terminal naming can live in labels, section titles, microinteractions, and visual framing, but the site should feel modern and premium more than hardcore technical.
